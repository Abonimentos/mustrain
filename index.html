📚 Training Science & Research

Evidence-based exercise physiology and training considerations

🎓

General Training Principles

Effective resistance training follows universal principles: progressive overload, adequate recovery, proper form, and consistency. These apply to all individuals regardless of sex.

  • Progressive Overload: Gradually increase weight, reps, or volume
  • Recovery: Allow 48-72 hours between training the same muscle group
  • Nutrition: Adequate protein (1.6-2.2g per kg body weight) and calories
  • Form: Proper technique prevents injury and maximizes results
👩

Female-Specific Training Considerations

While training principles are universal, female physiology presents unique considerations that can optimize training outcomes and prevent injury.

🦴 Biomechanical Differences

  • Wider Q-Angle: Increased hip width affects knee tracking; emphasize proper alignment in squats and lunges
  • Joint Laxity: Greater ligament elasticity requires focus on controlled movements and stability
  • Lower Body Strength: Naturally stronger legs relative to upper body; excellent for hip-dominant exercises
  • Pelvic Floor: Awareness during high-impact and heavy lifting exercises

⚠️ Injury Prevention Focus Areas

  • ACL Protection: 4-6x higher injury risk; prioritize hamstring strength and landing mechanics
  • Knee Alignment: Prevent valgus collapse (knees caving in) during squatting patterns
  • Core Stability: Essential for spinal protection and pelvic floor health
  • Shoulder Stability: Rotator cuff strengthening to prevent impingement

💪 Training Advantages

  • Fatigue Resistance: Higher Type I muscle fiber proportion allows for greater training volume
  • Recovery: Often faster recovery between sets due to metabolic differences
  • Endurance: Superior muscular endurance capabilities
  • Lower Body Power: Excellent glute and leg development potential

🔬 Hormonal Considerations

  • Menstrual Cycle: Performance may fluctuate; listen to your body
  • Follicular Phase (Days 1-14): Often optimal for high-intensity training
  • Luteal Phase (Days 15-28): May benefit from moderate intensity and volume
  • Individual Variation: Effects differ greatly; track your own patterns
